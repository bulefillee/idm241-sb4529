Microinteractions Project

- A Case Study by Sue Batham


Overview:

In the pursuit of creating a captivating and user-centric web interface, the IDM241 course project unfolded over 11 weeks, culminating in a dynamic and engaging final build. The project traversed three distinct stages, each adding layers of interactivity and visual appeal to the user experience. From the alpha stage, introducing a mesmerizing 3D flip effect, to the beta stage, where heart icon interactions conveyed user sentiments, and finally, the feature-rich final build, integrating a dynamic Windows icon and subtle hover effects. This case study delves into the thought process, challenges, and iterative enhancements that shaped the micro-interactions, transforming a static interface into a dynamic, emotionally resonant, and aesthetically pleasing user journey.


Context & Challenge:

Undertaking an 11-week web design project, the context was shaped by the challenge of refining and innovating upon Ubisoft's game card example, a well-crafted canvas in the digital space. The project's background involved a meticulous exploration of micro-interactions, with a keen focus on the final build phase. Aligned horizontally at the top of the webpage, the final micro-interactions aimed to harmonize aesthetics and functionality.

The challenge lay in strategically implementing and coding these micro-interactions to enhance user experience without deviating from the existing design language. The timeline of 11 weeks necessitated a phased approach, spanning alpha, beta, and final iterations, allowing for iterative improvements. The budget, in terms of time and resources, was allocated to facilitate creativity and coding precision. The purpose was not only to refine existing elements but to introduce triggers, rules, feedback mechanisms, and meta-rules that added depth and engagement to the user interface.

The problem addressed was the need for subtle yet impactful micro-interactions that would elevate user engagement and satisfaction. The goals and objectives revolved around creating a final product that not only met course requirements but exceeded expectations in terms of usability, aesthetics, and the seamless integration of micro-interactions. The success metrics were set to gauge user interaction, feedback, and overall satisfaction, aligning with the user-centric ethos of the project.


The Process & Insights:

  • Understanding the Context:
    The project began with a comprehensive analysis of the Ubisoft website's game card example, aiming to identify areas for improvement. The existing design served as a foundation, and the goal was to elevate user engagement while maintaining a seamless user experience.
  • Iterative Design:
    The iterative design process unfolded across three key stages: alpha, beta, and the final build. Each stage built upon the previous one, incorporating feedback from both the professor and classmates. The alpha stage focused on a 3D card flip, beta introduced a dynamic heart animation, and the final build integrated a floating windows icon and a captivating price hover effect.
  • User-Centric Approach:
    User feedback, particularly from the professor and classmates, played a pivotal role in shaping the micro-interactions. Insights gained during the alpha and beta stages informed refinements and adjustments in pursuit of an interface that would resonate with users.
  • Decision-Making and Enhancements:
    Decisions were guided by a desire to enhance existing elements. The 3D card flip aimed to bring an immersive feel, the heart animation sought to make favoriting more delightful, the windows icon highlighted platform availability, and the price hover effect drew attention to product details.
  • Balancing Aesthetics and Functionality:
    Throughout the process, the focus remained on striking a balance between aesthetics and functionality. Insights from the iterative design process allowed for continuous improvement, resulting in micro-interactions that not only met the project's objectives but also aligned with user expectations.
  • Harmonious Blend in the Final Build:
    The final build seamlessly integrated the refined micro-interactions, showcasing a harmonious blend of imaginative design and practical refinement. The culmination of insights and design decisions resulted in an interface that surpassed expectations, providing an engaging and user-centric experience.

"Desgining with Details" - Dan Saffer



The Solution:

The culmination of an 11-week solo design project resulted in a captivating and user-centric interface, evolving through three pivotal stages. The alpha stage introduced a mesmerizing 3D flip effect to the game card, laying the groundwork for an immersive and visually stimulating user experience. Progressing to the beta stage, user engagement was refined with a heart icon interaction, where the first click expressed affection by filling the heart icon with a blush pink color, smoothly transitioning to red and pulsating, signifying 'hearting' the game card. A second click elegantly un-heartened the game card, fading the red color and returning to a 'white outlined heart.' The final build stage added a touch of playfulness with a dynamic Windows icon animation and enhanced visual dynamics through subtle hover effects on the price area. This personal project, enriched by feedback from peers, resulted in a dynamic interface that showcased creative design thinking and delivered an engaging and satisfying user experience.

The Result:

View functioning Micro-interaction(s)

The culmination of the iterative design process led to a final product that successfully implemented user experience design principles, resulting in an interactive web page that exceeded expectations. The refined micro-interactions, influenced by feedback from the professor and classmates, contributed to a user-friendly interface that fostered meaningful engagement. The carefully crafted 3D flip for the game card, dynamic heart animation for favoriting, attention-grabbing windows icon, and price hover effect collectively enhanced the overall user experience. While meeting course objectives, the project showcased a thoughtful balance of functionality and aesthetics, providing users with an enjoyable and interactive platform for exploring game details.
